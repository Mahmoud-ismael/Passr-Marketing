Data Processing Agreement (DPA)

Pursuant to Article 28 of GDPR (EU) 2016/679

PREAMBLE: This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") forms part of the Terms of Service between the Customer (Data Controller) and Hisako Technologies GmbH / Passr (Data Processor). It governs the processing of personal data by Passr on behalf of the Customer to ensure compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

1. Definitions

Term Definition
Data Controller The Customer (the outdoor brand or business entity using Passr).
Data Processor Passr / Hisako Technologies GmbH.
Personal Data Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person (Art. 4(1) GDPR).
Processing Any operation performed on personal data, such as collection, storage, or disclosure (Art. 4(2) GDPR).
Sub-processor Any third party engaged by Passr to process personal data on behalf of the Customer.

2. Subject Matter and Duration

  • Subject: The subject matter of this DPA is the processing of personal data necessary to provide the Passr Digital Product Passport platform and related compliance services.
  • Duration: This DPA remains in effect for the duration of the Customer's active subscription to the Passr platform.

3. Nature and Purpose of Processing

Category Purpose Legal Basis
Brand Admin User Data Account management, secure authentication Contract Performance
Brand Team User Data Access control and collaborative editing Contract Performance
Passport Scan Events Analytics reporting for the Customer Legitimate Interest

4. Technical and Organisational Measures (TOMs)

Passr implements the following state-of-the-art security measures to protect personal data:

  • Encryption in Transit: Mandatory TLS 1.3 encryption for all data transmissions.
  • Encryption at Rest: AES-256 encryption for all database volumes and backups.
  • Access Control: Implementation of Row-Level Security (RLS) and Role-Based Access Control (RBAC).
  • Authentication: Secure JWT-based authentication with short-lived tokens and httpOnly cookies.
  • Backups: Daily automated backups stored within the EU (Frankfurt) with high availability.
  • Incident Response: Formalized internal runbooks for rapid security incident containment and remediation.
  • Employee Access: Strict adherence to the "Minimum Necessary Access" principle for internal systems.

5. Sub-Processors

Passr utilizes the sub-processors listed in our Privacy Policy. Passr ensures that all sub-processors are bound by data protection obligations equivalent to those in this DPA.

Notification: Passr will provide the Customer with at least 30 days' notice before adding any new sub-processors that will process Customer personal data, providing the Customer the opportunity to object on legitimate grounds.

6. Data Subject Rights

Passr shall assist the Customer, taking into account the nature of the processing, by implementing appropriate technical and organizational measures for the fulfillment of the Customer's obligation to respond to requests for exercising data subject rights (access, deletion, portability). Passr commits to responding to Customer inquiries regarding such requests within 72 hours.

7. Data Breach Notification

In accordance with Article 33 of the GDPR, Passr will notify the Customer without undue delay, and in any event within 72 hours, after becoming aware of a personal data breach affecting the Customer's personal data.

8. Deletion on Termination

Upon termination of the subscription and following the 30-day "Data Escrow" export period, Passr shall delete all Customer personal data from its production systems within 30 days, unless EU or member state law requires continued storage.

9. Audit Rights

The Customer may request, no more than once per year, evidence of Passr's compliance with its obligations under this DPA. Such requests will be satisfied through the provision of security audit summaries or third-party certifications.

10. Governing Law

This DPA shall be governed by the same jurisdiction as the Passr Terms of Service (Estonia).
